White Mountains Climate & Avalanche Research Center

Located in the heart of New Hampshire's White Mountains, our research center is dedicated to advancing the understanding of alpine climate systems and avalanche dynamics in the northeastern United States. Through rigorous scientific research, real-time monitoring, and community education, we work to enhance public safety and contribute to global climate science. Our team of experts combines cutting-edge technology with decades of field experience to study changing weather patterns, snowpack evolution, and their impacts on mountain ecosystems and communities.

Sarah Johnson

Lead Alpine Ski Instructor at Mountain High Resort

Sarah Johnson brings over a decade of professional skiing experience to Mountain High Resort's instructional team. After competing at the national level in alpine skiing throughout her college years, she discovered her true passion in teaching others to master the sport. Her innovative teaching methods and patient approach have helped hundreds of students, from first-time skiers to advanced athletes, develop their skills and confidence on the slopes.

As a certified Level 3 instructor with the Professional Ski Instructors of America (PSIA), Sarah specializes in both technical skill development and fostering a deep appreciation for mountain safety and environmental stewardship. When she's not teaching on the slopes, she dedicates her time to developing youth skiing programs and organizing women's skiing clinics, aiming to make the sport more accessible and enjoyable for underrepresented groups in the skiing community. Her commitment to excellence and infectious enthusiasm have made her one of the most sought-after instructors at Mountain High Resort.

John Smith

Co-founder of Mountain High Resort

John Smith has been a dedicated ski patroller at Mountain High Resort for over 15 years. His journey began as a competitive skier in his youth, but he found his true calling in helping others stay safe on the slopes. With extensive training in emergency medical services and avalanche rescue, John leads his team in conducting daily safety assessments and responding to emergencies in even the most challenging conditions.

Beyond his professional duties, John is passionate about educating the skiing community about mountain safety. He regularly conducts workshops for both beginners and advanced skiers, teaching them essential survival skills and proper skiing techniques. During the off-season, John maintains his medical certifications and participates in wilderness rescue training to ensure he's always prepared for the demands of the upcoming winter season.

John is a dedicated family man and a respected member of the ski community. He is known for his calm demeanor and quick thinking in emergency situations, often saving lives with his expertise and compassion.

Dr. Emily Chen

Senior Research Geologist at Mountain High Geological Survey

Dr. Emily Chen has dedicated her career to unraveling the complex geological history of mountain ranges across North America. With a Ph.D. in Structural Geology from Stanford University and over 15 years of field experience, she specializes in studying the formation and evolution of the Rocky Mountains. Her groundbreaking research on fault systems and rock deformation has contributed significantly to our understanding of mountain-building processes and has been published in numerous prestigious scientific journals.

Beyond her research work, Dr. Chen is passionate about making geology accessible to the public. She leads educational field trips for local schools and community groups, helping people understand the dramatic geological forces that shaped our landscape. Her innovative approach to science communication, combining traditional geological methods with modern technology, has earned her several awards for public outreach. When not in the field or laboratory, she mentors young female scientists and advocates for increased diversity in the geosciences through her role as director of the Women in Geoscience Initiative.

Dr. Michael Rodriguez

Senior Climatologist at Mountain High Climate Research Center

Dr. Michael Rodriguez is a distinguished climatologist with over 20 years of experience studying mountain climate systems and their response to global environmental changes. After earning his doctorate from the University of Colorado Boulder, he has focused his research on high-altitude weather patterns, snowpack analysis, and long-term climate trends in alpine environments. His innovative work using advanced climate modeling techniques has helped predict seasonal weather patterns crucial for ski resort operations and has contributed to our understanding of how climate change affects mountain ecosystems.

As the lead researcher at Mountain High's Climate Research Center, Dr. Rodriguez oversees a network of weather monitoring stations and coordinates with international climate research institutions. His dedication to climate science education has made him a frequent speaker at environmental conferences and local schools, where he engages audiences with compelling presentations about climate science and conservation. Outside of his research duties, he serves on several environmental advisory boards and actively works with local governments to develop climate adaptation strategies for mountain communities. His commitment to combining rigorous scientific research with practical applications has made him an invaluable resource for both the scientific community and mountain resort operations.
